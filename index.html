About the Project

Engineering meets international collaboration

The International BUSIT Week brought students from across Europe together for five days of hands-on engineering. The challenge: design, develop and race an autonomous smart car — all within one week.

Teams worked with Raspberry Pi-powered vehicles, custom sensors and autonomous driving logic, while collaborating across language and cultural boundaries.

Final Reflection

What we built was more than a car

"Five days is not a long time — yet within those five days, we designed, built, tested and raced an autonomous smart car as an international team."

The project challenged us in ways that a standard classroom exercise cannot replicate. Debugging a non-responsive sensor at the end of a long day, negotiating technical approaches across language barriers, making decisions under time pressure — these are the conditions that real engineering demands.

We left with stronger technical skills in embedded systems and autonomous control. We left with better communication abilities, developed through five days of working exclusively in English. And we left with a deeper appreciation for international collaboration — something no lecture can fully teach.

The Smart Car Race was the culmination of the week, but the most important result was demonstrating that an international team, meeting for the first time, can produce something genuinely meaningful in five days.
